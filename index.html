About Me

I am a second-year Ph.D. student in Transportation Engineering at the University of Florida, where I am also pursuing a Ph.D. minor in Urban and Regional Planning. I completed both my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Transportation Engineering from Pulchowk Campus, Tribhuvan University, Nepal. My doctoral research is currently being supervised by Dr. Sivaramakrishnan Srinivasan.

My research is centered on improving transportation safety and planning through data-driven methodologies. Specifically, I focus on:
a) Identifying and understanding the causal factors behind various types of traffic crashes;
b) Developing and applying statistical and machine learning models to examine the spatial and temporal dynamics of crash occurrences;

In addition to crash analysis, my broader interests include big data analytics, safety performance modeling, travel behavior analysis, and transportation planning. I frequently work with discrete choice models and other statistical as well as Machine Learning techniques to examine behavioral responses and guide data-driven decision-making in transportation systems.

In my free time, I like to read books (fiction and non-fiction both), play chess and watch football.
